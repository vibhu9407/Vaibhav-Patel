Ahmedabad:India's first UNESCO world heritage city

"A journey through history & culture"

Ahmedabad is a beautiful puzzle of old and new. Founded in 1411, it is famous for its narrow street alleys, traditional wooden homes called havelis and its deep connection to Mahatma Gandhi's peace movement. Today, it is a bustling modern citythat proudly celebrates its rich Gujarati identity throughlively festivals, stunning textiles, and local entrepreneurship.

"Must visit iconic landmarks"

The city is filled with structural wonders that span centuries. From ancient underground stepwells built with intricate hand-carved stone to ultra-modern, colorful footbridges over the Sabarmati River, Ahmedabad offers a spectacular visual journey. These sites tell the stories of kings, freedom fighters, and visionary modern architects.

"The peacful Sabarmati ashram"

  • Located peacefully on the banks of the Sabarmati River.
  • Served as the historic home and headquarters of Mahatma Gandhi.
  • Functions today as a museum displaying personal letters and spinning wheels.

"The Architectural Marvel of Adalaj Stepwell"

  • Built over 500 years ago to store rainwater and provide shade.
  • Features a unique five-story underground design with cool air.

"The Futuristic Atal Pedestrian Bridge"

  • A modern glass-and-steel footbridge built for pedestrians.
  • Inspired by the colorful kites of the famous Uttarayan festival.
  • Vibrantly lit up at night to create a beautiful river skyline.
